Pocket Racer Team

It's safe to say that building autonomous vehicles is a difficult task to be completed by one person alone. Meet the team of individuals who have come together to align their passions and innovate technology.

Dr. Jesse Cha

Principal Investigator

My name is Eun Sang (Jesse) Cha, and I am a University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) Mechanical Engineering PhD student based in Bel Air, Los Angeles.

As a roboticist, my research focuses on high-performance autonomous driving using intelligent control and imitation learning-based methods. I utilize miniature autonomous vehicular platforms for teaching data science and conducting research in transportation systems and robotic AI.

Junu Choe

Eric Laukien

My name is Eric Laukien, I've been researching off-the-beaten-path machine learning algorithms for over 10 years. After originally programming various Deep Learning algorithm implementations during its inception, I became convinced that machine learning algorithms should learn fully online, and that one of the best ways of achieving that was to mimic biology.

To this end, I co-founded Ogma Corp in 2016, and have since then been working on a novel technology known as Sparse Predictive Hierarchies as a basis for machine intelligence, where online learning is front and center.

Along the way, I created several demonstrations of this technology, focusing heavily on robotics applications and low-compute devices.

Myron McClure

My name is Myron McClure, I am a Southern California native, a man of faith, and a former All-American track & field athlete. I use my servant leadership approach to provide expertise and consulting activities around the engagement of DEI spaces and to build culturally competent and culturally humble leaders for policy change. I hope to serve others to see the change they want to see.
